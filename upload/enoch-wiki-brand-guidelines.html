01 — Foundations

Brand essence

We hold the craft standard of a best-in-class editorial product — Substack-level clarity, generous whitespace, one decisive accent, and type-and-color treated as a single system — and express it through a calm, scholarly, navy-and-gold identity. The feeling is an illuminated manuscript meets a modern research tool: authoritative without being cold, reverent without being mystical.

01

Content first

The text and its evidence are the hero. Chrome recedes; reading comes first. Whitespace is a feature, not an absence.

02

One decisive accent

Gold is used sparingly and meaningfully — the mark, a single key action, a highlight. Restraint is what makes it feel premium.

03

Credible, not cryptic

Scholarly and luminous, never occult or sensational. Every claim shows its sources and its certainty. Trust is the product.

04

A true system

Color, type, and space share one visual language. Consistency across every page is what reads as quality.

05 — Iconography & imagery

Icons & imagery

Icon style

Thin-to-medium line icons on a consistent stroke (≈2px at 24px). Geometric, calm, single-color (indigo, or gold for accent). Motifs drawn from the brand world: stars, orbital rings, scrolls, codices, keys, lamps.

Imagery

Do: high-resolution manuscript folios (Ge'ez script, the Garima Gospels), archival illuminations, astronomical plates, paper and vellum textures — treated calmly, with room to breathe.

Avoid: sensational "ancient mystery" stock, glowing aliens, zodiac kitsch, or dramatic CGI. The imagery must earn the same trust as the writing.

08 — Voice & tone

Voice & tone

Scholarly but warm. Plain-spoken, precise, and honest about uncertainty. We are a calm, well-read guide — never a hype machine, never a preacher, never a conspiracy feed.

✓ Do

  • Lead with what the text actually says, then what's corroborated.
  • Name the source and label the claim type, every time.
  • Distinguish fact, scholarship, tradition, and speculation in plain words.
  • Be reverent toward the material and neutral toward belief.
“1 Enoch is not in most Bibles, but the Ethiopian Orthodox canon preserved it. Here's what it says — and where scholars agree.”

✕ Don't

  • Hype, clickbait, or manufactured mystery.
  • Present contested or devotional claims as settled fact.
  • Flatten denominations or talk down to readers.
  • Borrow the aesthetics of the "forbidden knowledge" internet.
“The book they BANNED from the Bible reveals the SHOCKING truth they don't want you to know…”

09 — Accessibility

Accessibility

Contrast

All text meets WCAG AA or better. Text is indigo on light or white on indigo. Gold never carries text on light backgrounds (2.46:1).

Focus & targets

Visible focus rings (2px indigo, 2px offset). Interactive targets ≥ 44px. Never rely on color alone — tags carry text and a dot.

Reading

Body ≥ 17px, long-form 19px, line height ≥ 1.6, measure ≤ 68ch. Respect prefers-reduced-motion.

Manuscripts

Every folio/illustration needs descriptive alt text. Ge'ez quotations are marked with lang="gez" and given transliteration.
